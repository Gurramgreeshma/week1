Brindavan Gardens

Brindavan Gardens is a renowned garden situated in Mysore, India. The garden is well-known for its stunning water fountains, which are illuminated with vivid lights at night and draw both tourists and locals to the area. The attractiveness of this garden is enhanced by the well-kept lawns, radiant flowers, and exquisite plants, making it the ideal location for taking memorable photos and spending time with your family and friends.

Brindavan Gardens One of the most popular things to do in the Brindavan Gardens is to witness the entrancing musical fountain show. The show is held in the evenings, making it one of the most captivating sights in the garden. A wonderful and mesmerising atmosphere is created by the fountain show, which is accompanied by music and light displays. It is a must-see site in the Brindavan Gardens which offers a delightful experience for visitors of all ages. The North Brindavan area of the Brindavan Gardens is considered to be the most appealing section due to its impressive four large terrace gardens. Each of these terrace gardens offers a stunning view of flourishing flower beds, lush green lawns, decorated hedges, and ornamental trees. Additionally, visitors can also find an ornamental plant nursery located in the area. The Brindavan Gardens are known for their famous musical fountains, which are powered by the water pressure from the dam and are controlled by a controller. The musical fountain show presents a synchronised dance of water with colourful lights and music. The North Brindavan area is where this show takes place and a special rain shelter and gallery have been constructed for visitors. The garden includes everything, from recreational water activities to spiritual locations. The garden is a haven from the city’s noise and bustle, and you’ll be entranced by its tranquility and beauty. Brindavan Gardens Horticulture Farms There are two horticulture farms at the Brindavan Garden in Mysore. The Naguvana Horticulture Farm spread over an area of 30 acre, is situated near the main gate. It features various types of plants and houses the Horticulture Training Centre of the Department of Horticulture. The centre provides trainings to the local youth. The Chandravana Horticulture Farm is a relatively smaller farm situated in the heart of River Cauvery. It is spread over an area of around five acre. The park features succulent mango trees and coconut trees.
